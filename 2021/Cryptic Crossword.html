Brief Tutorial on Cryptic Crossword - Part 1

Date Tue 07 September 2021 Tags Crossword

Defintion of Cryptic Crossword as per Cambridge Dictionary is "a type of crossword (= word game) that has difficult clues (= pieces of information) that are not obvious in their meaning". Cryptic crossword clues are normally in three parts - A straight clue (Definition), the answer hidden somehow(Wordplay) and words that indicate how it is hidden(Indicator). These are made into a sentence, not necessarily in that order, that sort of makes sense literally but usually the wrong sense. The best way to solve a cryptic clue is to see if you can identify the straight clue(Definition). Definition is always either at first (word or phrase) or last (again word or phrase).

The beauty of cryptic crossword clues is that you know when you have the correct answer, not like a normal crossword.

Let us take a few examples.

Outlaw taking shelter in mountain kingdom (6)

Here straight clue or definition part is mountain kingdom which can be both locations as well as singular Nouns. The answer is hidden in outlaw taking shelter which is also called wordplay.in is indicative of how answer is hidden in wordplay part ie outlaw taking Shelter.

'outlaw' becomes 'ban' (banning is a kind of outlawing).

'taking' indicates putting letters inside.

'shelter' becomes 'hut' (hut is a kind of shelter).

'ban' going around 'hut' is 'BHUTAN'.

Noises in restless slumber (7)

Here again definition part is Noises and restless indicates that we have to use Anagram to find the answer. Anagram of Slumber is rumbles which means 'a continuous low sound'.

Now we outline a few basic tricks used in solving Cryptic Crossword. In part 1 Double Definition, Anagram and Hidden Answers is Covered. In Part 2 Homophones, Charades, Containers and Reversals will be covered.

DOUBLE DEFINITIONS

The simplest kind of wordplay hint in a clue is a second definition. For example, HOOD can mean “gangster” or “a cover for the head.” So a clue for HOOD might read:

Cover for the head gangster (4)

Baby cow's leg part (4)

CALF is both Baby Cow and Lower Part of Leg

Hawthorn blossom's month (3)

MAY is the flowers of the hawthorn tree and also a Month.

Abandon the wasteland (6)

DESERT means Abandon as well as wasteland.

Loud noise from a tennis bat (6)

Racket is a tennis bat as well as an unpleasant continuous noise.

ANAGRAMS

Anagram indicator is a word, or sometimes a brief phrase, indicating that some of the letters of words in the clue need to be jumbled up and rearranged, to find the answer.

So, an anagram indicator can be pretty much any word that gives a sense of things being broken, muddled, jumbled, built, confused, insane, cooked, mixed, damaged, upset, or even drunk!

Insane damn yeti is explosive (8)

insane is an Anagram Indicator. So we look for synonym of explosive with rearrangement of damn yeti and we find Dynamite which is explosive.

He cooked planet's animal (8)

This clue gives examples of a couple of cryptic tricks. Firstly, the anagram indicator (cooked) is in the middle of the fodder, not at one end. And secondly, the apostrophe s at the end of planet is not a possessive apostrophe, showing ownership (ie the animal belonging to the planet), but is a contraction of is = it's effectively saying "A cooked version of he + planet is a word for a type of animal". It is Elephant.

Badly pare the fruit (4)

Badly is Anagram indicator and we get a fruit Pear with rearrangement of pare.

Flustered, I forget rarer chilly compartment (12)

As the definition has to be at the start or end of the clue, that means it's probably flustered or chilly compartment, or just compartment. However, that flustered does look a bit like an anagram indicator, so let's see if some of the other words in the clue add up to 12 letters. How about "I forget rarer?" This gives us Refrigrator - that's a chilly compartment.

Perilous sea dog? Run all over the place! (9)

When first looking at the this clue, see if we can spot the anagram indicator ... it's all over the place. Now, the answer is 9 letters long. What collection of words in the clue add up to 9 letters? Sea dog run works. Now make them go all over the place! Perilous is the definition. This clue highlights another point about cryptics — in general, ignore the punctuation! Sea dog runs becomes Dangerous.

Noises in restless slumber (7)

restless is Anagram indicator and we get Rumbles from Slumber which mean Continuous noise.

HIDDEN ANSWERS

Here the answer will be hidden inside a longer word or phrase eg Appeal to you to get through an apple a day (5) (as PLEAD is tucked inside “apPLE A Day” and meaning Appeal).Indicators such as “caught in,” “buried in,” “part of”,"get through" and “housed by.”

Herb's buried in a filTHY MEadow (5) Thyme is a herb.

Pilfer a bit of waSTE ALexandrite (5) STEAL means pilfer.

TAPEStry covers primates (4)

Enjoyment from malFUNction (3)

Rug held by derMATologist (3)
